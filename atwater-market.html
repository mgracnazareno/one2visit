Atwater Market

Located in the heart of the Sud-Ouest borough, Atwater Market is one of Montreal's most beloved public markets—buzzing with local flavor, colorful stalls, and fresh food all year long.

Opened in 1933, the market is housed in a beautiful Art Deco building and is named after the nearby Atwater Avenue. It’s a go-to destination for seasonal produce, specialty cheeses, meats, pastries, and flowers.

Local farmers and merchants offer high-quality goods—from Quebec maple syrup and berries in summer, to artisanal sausages, baked goods, and holiday trees in winter. It’s a favorite stop for locals shopping for dinner and visitors discovering authentic Montreal tastes.

The market also hosts cafés, ready-to-eat counters, and specialty shops. Its proximity to the Lachine Canal makes it a perfect place to grab a picnic before heading out on a walk or bike ride.

  • 🕒 Hours: Mon–Fri 9AM–6PM, Sat 9AM–5PM, Sun 9AM–5PM (hours may vary by vendor)

Best Time to Visit

The market is lively on weekends, especially during spring to fall. Weekday mornings are quieter if you're looking for a more relaxed experience. Fall harvest season is particularly colorful and festive!
