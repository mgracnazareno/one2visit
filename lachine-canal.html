Lachine Canal

Stretching 14.5 km through the southwest of Montreal, the Lachine Canal is a scenic urban oasis that connects the city's industrial past with outdoor fun and local culture.

Originally opened in 1825, the canal played a vital role in Montreal’s economic development. Today, it's a bustling recreational corridor where locals and tourists enjoy everything from biking and running to kayaking and guided boat tours.

The canal’s multi-use path connects neighborhoods like Griffintown, St-Henri, and Lachine. Along the way, you’ll discover historic locks, heritage interpretation panels, artisan cafés, art installations, and lush green picnic spots.

Kayak and paddleboard rentals are available at multiple points, and Parks Canada offers information stations and occasional guided heritage walks during summer months. Winter visitors can also enjoy cross-country skiing along the frozen trail.

  • 📍 Address: Rue Saint-Patrick, Montreal, QC (from Old Port to Lake Saint-Louis)
  • 🕒 Hours: Open year-round. Lock operations: May to October, 9 AM – 5 PM
  • 🚲 Bike Rentals: Atwater Market & Old Port access points
  • 🔗 Website: pc.gc.ca/canallachine

Best Time to Visit

The Lachine Canal is most vibrant from late spring to early fall, especially during weekends when events and activities are at their peak. Sunrise and sunset hours offer the most picturesque views for photography lovers.
