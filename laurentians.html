Explore the Laurentian Mountains

The Laurentians (Les Laurentides) are a spectacular mountain range just north of Montreal, known for their breathtaking natural landscapes, charming villages, and four-season adventures.

With dense forests, crystal-clear lakes, and scenic valleys, this region offers endless outdoor activities—skiing and snowboarding in winter, hiking and cycling in summer, and brilliant fall foliage displays that attract visitors from around the world.

Discover quaint towns like Mont-Tremblant, Sainte-Adèle, and Saint-Sauveur, each with its own personality and local delights. From luxury spas and gourmet dining to rustic lodges and farmers' markets, the Laurentians are a true Quebec gem.

  • 📍 Location: North of Montreal, Quebec
  • 🕒 Best Time to Visit: Year-round (skiing in winter, hiking in summer, foliage in fall)
  • 🔗 Tourism Site: laurentides.com
